‚Ä¢ Training Load Management
  Why it matters: Triathlon training involves managing fatigue across three sports simultaneously. Without proper recovery tracking, overtraining injuries are common.
  Impact: Watches with HRV, sleep tracking, and training stress scores help prevent burnout and optimize performance gains.

‚Ä¢ Open Water GPS Accuracy
  Why it matters: Pool swimming vs open water requires different GPS technologies. Many watches lose signal accuracy in water, affecting distance and pace data critical for race pacing.
  Impact: Dual-frequency GPS and specific open water modes are essential for accurate triathlon data. This eliminates Garmin 265 and Apple Watch as top choices.

‚Ä¢ Quick Band Interchange System
  Why it matters: Transitioning from sweaty training sessions to professional meetings requires different band styles. Standard pin systems are slow and cumbersome.
  Impact: Quick-release systems and availability of both sport and professional bands determine real-world usability in dual office/training environments.

‚Ä¢ Structured Workout Programming
  Why it matters: Triathlon training requires following specific interval workouts across three sports. Without on-watch workout guidance, users rely on external devices or memory.
  Impact: Built-in workout builder and coach integration (TrainingPeaks, etc.) transforms the watch from data recorder to active training partner.

‚Ä¢ Recovery Time Algorithms
  Why it matters: Unlike single-sport training, triathlon requires understanding which muscle groups have recovered for the next workout type (swim after hard run, etc.).
  Impact: Advanced recovery metrics help optimize training schedule and prevent injury, crucial for athletes balancing intense training with professional work stress.
